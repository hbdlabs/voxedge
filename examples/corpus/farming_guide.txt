Maize is the most important food crop in many African countries. Plant maize at the start of the rainy season when the soil is moist. Space plants 75 cm between rows and 25 cm between plants. Apply compost or manure before planting to improve soil fertility. Weed the field twice: first at 2 weeks and again at 5 weeks after planting. Harvest when the husks turn brown and the kernels are hard.

Composting turns farm waste into valuable fertilizer. Collect crop residues, animal manure, kitchen scraps, and dry leaves. Build a pile in layers: green material, then dry material, then a thin layer of soil. Keep the pile moist but not wet. Turn it every 2 weeks. Compost is ready when it is dark, crumbly, and smells like earth, usually after 2 to 3 months. Apply compost around plants or mix it into the soil before planting.

Chickens are a good source of income and nutrition for rural families. A small flock of 10 to 20 hens can provide eggs daily and meat. Feed chickens grain, kitchen scraps, and insects. Provide clean water every day. Build a simple coop to protect them from predators at night. Vaccinate against Newcastle disease, which is the most common killer of village chickens. Collect eggs daily and store them in a cool, dry place.

Drip irrigation saves water and increases crop yields. It delivers water directly to the roots of plants through tubes with small holes. You can build a simple drip system using a raised water tank, a filter, and perforated hoses. Drip irrigation uses 30 to 50 percent less water than flood irrigation and reduces weed growth. It is especially useful for vegetable gardens and during the dry season.

Soil health is the foundation of good farming. Test your soil every 2 years to check nutrient levels and acidity. Rotate crops each season to prevent nutrient depletion and reduce pest problems. Legumes like beans and groundnuts fix nitrogen in the soil and improve fertility for the next crop. Avoid burning crop residues — instead, leave them on the field as mulch to protect the soil from erosion and retain moisture.
