Malaria is a serious disease spread by mosquitoes. Prevention includes sleeping under insecticide-treated bed nets, using mosquito repellent, and taking antimalarial medication. Symptoms include fever, chills, headache, and body aches. If you suspect malaria, visit your nearest health clinic immediately. Early treatment with artemisinin-based combination therapy is effective.

Diarrhea is one of the leading causes of death in children under 5. It is caused by contaminated water or food. To prevent diarrhea, always wash your hands with soap before eating and after using the toilet. Boil drinking water if you are unsure of its safety. If a child has diarrhea, give oral rehydration salts (ORS) mixed with clean water immediately. Continue breastfeeding. Seek medical help if diarrhea lasts more than 3 days or if the child shows signs of dehydration.

Vaccines protect children from dangerous diseases. The WHO recommends that all children receive vaccines for tuberculosis (BCG), polio, diphtheria, tetanus, pertussis (DPT), measles, hepatitis B, and Haemophilus influenzae type b (Hib). Vaccination should begin at birth and follow the national immunization schedule. Vaccines are safe, free at government health centers, and save millions of lives every year.

Pregnant women should attend at least 4 antenatal care visits. During these visits, health workers check blood pressure, test for infections, provide iron and folic acid supplements, and monitor the baby's growth. Delivery should take place at a health facility with a skilled birth attendant. Danger signs during pregnancy include severe headache, blurred vision, swelling of hands and face, and vaginal bleeding. If any of these occur, go to the hospital immediately.

Clean water and sanitation prevent many diseases. Always use a latrine or toilet instead of open defecation. Wash hands with soap at five critical times: after using the toilet, after cleaning a child, before preparing food, before eating, and before feeding a child. Store drinking water in a clean, covered container. If water is not from a safe source, boil it for at least one minute before drinking.
