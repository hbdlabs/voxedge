Every citizen has the right to vote in national and local elections. To vote, you must be at least 18 years old and registered with the electoral commission. Registration is free. Bring your national ID card or birth certificate to the nearest registration center. Voting is by secret ballot. No one can force you to vote for a particular candidate. Election day is a public holiday.

Birth registration is important for every child. Register your child within 30 days of birth at the local civil registry office. You will need the hospital birth notification or a witness if the birth was at home. Birth registration is free. A birth certificate is required for school enrollment, accessing health services, and proving citizenship. If you missed the 30-day window, late registration is possible but may require additional documents.

Land rights protect farmers and families. If you own or use land, register it with the local land office. Bring your land sale agreement, witnesses, and identification. Registration costs a small fee but protects against land disputes. Women have equal rights to own and inherit land. Community land can be registered collectively. Keep copies of all land documents in a safe place.

Government health services are available at health centers and hospitals. Basic services are free, including malaria treatment, vaccinations, antenatal care, and treatment of common illnesses. Bring your health card to every visit. If your area does not have a health center, community health workers can provide basic care and referrals. Emergency services are available 24 hours at district hospitals.

Education is free and compulsory for all children from age 6 to 14. Register your child at the nearest primary school with their birth certificate. Schools provide free textbooks and learning materials. If a child has a disability, they have the right to attend school and receive appropriate support. Secondary school is subsidized by the government. Scholarships are available for students from low-income families — ask at the school or district education office.
